History

Rapids water rafting, also known as white-water rafting, has a rich history dating back to the early 19th century. The first recorded rafting expedition took place in 1811 when American explorers attempted to navigate the Snake River in Wyoming. However, without proper equipment or knowledge, their efforts were unsuccessful.

The modern sport of white-water rafting began in the mid-20th century when inflatable rafts were developed for military use during World War II. After the war, these durable rafts became popular for recreational use, and by the 1970s, rafting had gained worldwide recognition as an adventure sport.
